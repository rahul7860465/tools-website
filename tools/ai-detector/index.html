How to Use
  1. Paste the text you want to analyze.
  2. Click Detect Text.
  3. Review AI% and Human% scores.
  4. Use result as directional guidance, not absolute proof.

      
Example
AI Detector gives a quick estimate of whether text appears machine-generated. It is useful for editorial review, academic draft checks, and quality workflows where teams want to inspect consistency and writing patterns. The detector does not claim perfect certainty; instead, it provides directional probability based on measurable traits like repetition, sentence uniformity, transition predictability, and vocabulary variance.
In real projects, teams often receive mixed content from humans and AI tools. A probability score helps prioritize manual review by flagging highly repetitive text. Editors can then focus on sections that may need stronger voice, more concrete details, and better storytelling cadence. This saves time when auditing long drafts, outreach templates, or documentation where style quality matters.
This tool is intentionally lightweight and fast. It runs client-side and gives immediate feedback, which is ideal for early-stage screening. If content is business critical, use the result as one signal among others such as factual accuracy, originality, and tone consistency. Combining heuristic detection with human review usually produces better outcomes than relying on one method alone.
To get the most accurate estimate, analyze full paragraphs instead of one-line snippets. Very short text can be ambiguous and may distort probability. You can also compare multiple sections from the same article to see pattern consistency. Large differences between sections often indicate mixed writing sources or uneven editing quality.
AI Detector supports practical decisions in publishing workflows: where to edit first, where to add human examples, and where to refine tone for audience trust. It is best used as a fast guidance tool that helps teams improve final output quality.
Use the percentage output as an editorial trigger rather than a final judgment. If AI probability is high, strengthen specificity by adding real examples, unique experience, and clearer argument flow. That approach usually improves authenticity and reader trust, which is the ultimate goal for high-quality writing.
